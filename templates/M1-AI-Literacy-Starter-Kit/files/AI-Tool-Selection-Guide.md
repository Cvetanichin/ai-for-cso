# AI Tool Selection Guide for CSOs

*Part of the "AI Literacy for NGOs: Starter Kit" — Cvetanichin Consultancy*

Ten free or low-cost AI tools relevant to CSO workflows, with honest pros, cons, and best-fit use cases.

| Tool | Best For | Cost | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Claude** | Drafting, summarizing, M&E analysis | Free tier / paid | Strong reasoning, more privacy-conscious by default, handles long documents well | Paid tiers needed for heavy use |
| **ChatGPT** | General drafting, brainstorming | Free tier / paid | Widely known, broad plugin ecosystem | Data retention varies by plan — check settings |
| **Google Sheets + AI add-ons** | Quick data analysis, donor tracking | Free | Already familiar to most staff, low learning curve | Limited for complex qualitative analysis |
| **Canva (AI features)** | Reports, social media, donor decks | Free tier / paid | Easy for non-designers, brand-consistent templates | AI image features can misrepresent real beneficiaries — use carefully |
| **Notion AI** | Internal docs, meeting notes, knowledge bases | Paid add-on | Good for organisation-wide prompt/knowledge libraries | Added cost on top of Notion subscription |
| **Otter.ai** | Meeting transcription, stakeholder interviews | Free tier / paid | Saves significant time on M&E interview write-ups | Accuracy drops with poor audio or strong accents |
| **Grammarly** | Donor report polish, compliance language | Free tier / paid | Fast proofing for English-language donor reports | Not a substitute for substantive review |
| **DeepL** | Translating donor reports, multilingual outreach | Free tier / paid | More accurate than most free translators for EU languages | Domain-specific terminology may need manual correction |
| **Perplexity AI** | Donor research, funding landscape scans | Free tier / paid | Cites sources, useful for live donor mapping | Always verify cited sources directly before using in a proposal |
| **Zapier / Make** | Automating repetitive workflows (e.g. form-to-report) | Free tier / paid | Removes manual repetitive steps once set up | Setup requires some upfront time investment |

## How to Choose

1. Start with the task, not the tool — match the table above to your actual bottleneck.
2. Run any candidate tool through the **AI Risk Assessment Checklist** (included in this kit) before adopting it.
3. Pilot with one team member before rolling out organisation-wide.
4. Re-evaluate every six months — this space moves fast.
