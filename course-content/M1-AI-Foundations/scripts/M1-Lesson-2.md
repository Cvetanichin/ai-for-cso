## [SECTION 1: The Four-Step Prompt Formula 2:00–6:00]

**Visual:** Diagram of the four-step formula: Role, Task, Context, Constraints.

**Script:**
> **Step 1 — Role.** Assign the AI a specific role. AI adapts its tone and expertise to the role.
> - Weak: "Write a grant proposal." The AI doesn't know who it's writing for.
> - Strong: "You are a grant writer for a youth employment NGO."
>
> **Step 2 — Task.** State clearly what you want done. Vague tasks produce vague outputs.
> - Weak: "Help me with a report."
> - Strong: "Draft a one-page progress report for our EU-funded project."
>
> **Step 3 — Context.** Provide background — NGO type, region, donor, project details.
> - Weak: "Summarize the data."
> - Strong: "Summarize this beneficiary feedback data from our rural education project in Albania."
>
> **Step 4 — Constraints.** Add rules — word limit, tone, format.
> - Weak: "Write a social media post."
> - Strong: "Write a Twitter post (280 characters) in a friendly tone for our #YouthEmployment campaign."
>
> Putting it together:
> *"You are a communications officer for a human rights NGO working in North Macedonia. Draft a 200-word blog post about our gender equality project. Focus on impact for 200+ women and use simple language. Include a call-to-action for donations."*

## [SECTION 2: Live Demo — Optimizing a Prompt 6:00–10:00]

**Visual:** Screen recording of Claude with side-by-side comparisons.

**Script:**
> Scenario: you need a problem statement for a €50K EU grant on digital literacy for rural schools in Albania.
>
> Weak prompt: "Write a problem statement." Output: generic, irrelevant, too broad.
>
> Strong prompt (four-step formula): "You are a grant writer for an education NGO working in Albania. Draft a problem statement for a €50K EU-funded project on digital literacy for rural schools. Use data from this report. Keep it under 200 words and align with EU priorities for digital inclusion."
>
> Output: specific, data-driven, donor-aligned — directly usable.
>
> Key takeaway: the four-step formula turns vague requests into actionable, high-quality outputs.

## [SECTION 3: Common Prompt Mistakes 10:00–13:00]

**Visual:** Table of weak vs. strong prompts.

**Script:**
> **Mistake 1 — Too vague.** Fix: add specificity (what, for whom, why).
>
> **Mistake 2 — No context.** Fix: provide background on the data or task.
>
> **Mistake 3 — Overly complex.** Fix: simplify, focus on one goal at a time.
>
> **Mistake 4 — Missing constraints.** Fix: add format, tone, and length.
>
> **Mistake 5 — Ignoring the audience.** Fix: specify who the output is for — donor, beneficiary, partner.
>
> Pro tip: use the Prompt Optimizer web app from your Starter Kit to apply the four-step formula automatically.

## [SECTION 4: Testing and Refining Prompts 13:00–14:00]

**Script:**
> Even good prompts need tweaking.
>
> 1. Start simple — write a basic prompt using the four-step formula.
> 2. Run it and review the output. Is it specific enough? Does it align with your goal?
> 3. Refine — if too generic, add context; if too long, add a word limit; if off-topic, clarify the role or task.
> 4. Save successful prompts in a shared library (use the Notion template from your Starter Kit).
