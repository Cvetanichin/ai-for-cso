## [SECTION 1: The Three Biggest AI Risks for CSOs 1:00–4:00]

**Script:**
> **Risk 1 — Data privacy and security.** AI tools often store your data on third-party servers. Pasting sensitive beneficiary data into a consumer chatbot can expose it or feed it back into model training. CSO impact: GDPR exposure, loss of donor and beneficiary trust.
>
> **Risk 2 — Bias and discrimination.** AI models can replicate biases present in their training data. An AI tool screening grant applications might unintentionally favor urban applicants over rural ones. CSO impact: exclusion of marginalized groups, reputational damage.
>
> **Risk 3 — Over-reliance.** Depending on AI for critical decisions — grant allocations, beneficiary selection — without human review. CSO impact: missed opportunities, loss of human judgment in decisions that matter.

---

## [SECTION 2: How to Mitigate AI Risks 4:00–7:00]

**Script:**
> **Mitigating data privacy risk:** use privacy-conscious tools, avoid inputting personally identifiable or confidential donor data, and check each tool's data retention policy before adopting it.
>
> **Mitigating bias:** manually audit AI outputs for skew, especially in screening or scoring contexts; if using custom models, ensure training data is representative.
>
> **Mitigating over-reliance:** keep a human in the loop on any AI-assisted decision that affects beneficiaries or funding; train your team on where AI's judgment ends and yours begins.

## [SECTION 3: The AI Risk Assessment Checklist 7:00–9:00]

**Script:**
> Run every new AI tool through this checklist before adopting it — also included as a standalone download in your Starter Kit.
>
> **Data privacy:**
> - Does the tool store my data on third-party servers? If yes, can I opt out?
> - Does the tool comply with GDPR?
> - Will I be inputting sensitive data (PII, confidential reports)? If yes, avoid this tool or use a privacy-focused alternative.
>
> **Bias:**
> - Has the tool been tested for bias in my sector? Look for third-party audits.
> - Can I manually review AI outputs before they affect a decision?
>
> **Over-reliance:**
> - Will this tool be used for critical decisions? If yes, add a mandatory human review step.
> - Do I have a manual backup process if the tool fails?
>
> **Ethics:**
> - Does the tool align with my NGO's values?
> - Have I consulted my team before adopting it?
>
> Rule of thumb: if you answer "no" to any of the first three data-privacy questions, do not use the tool.
